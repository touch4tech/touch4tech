    Slack and long term survival

    Navigating the Tightrope: The Risks of Over-Optimization and the Strategic Benefits of Maintaining Slack in Systems

    Abstract

    In the relentless pursuit of efficiency, contemporary systems engineering and management practices often prioritize optimization, seeking to maximize resource utilization and minimize waste. However, this approach inherently entails risks that can undermine system resilience, adaptability, and long-term survival. This paper explores the dichotomy between the drive for optimization and the necessity of maintaining slack within systems, offering a comprehensive review of the potential pitfalls associated with over-optimization, including increased vulnerability to unexpected disturbances, reduced innovation, and the erosion of system resilience.
